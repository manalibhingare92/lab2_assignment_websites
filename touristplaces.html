Tourist Places in Pune

Agakhan Palace

Spread over 13 acres, the palace itself occupies seven acres. The palace is beautiful in its architecture with many Islamic influences with a touch of Italian architecture. The building comprises five halls and large verandahs. In March 2003, Aga Khan Palace was declared a ‘monument of national importance’ by the Archaeological Survey Of India. The palace is also popular for couples looking to get great pre-wedding photoshoots done.

Shanivarwada

Built in 1732, it was the great seat of the Peshwas of the Maratha Empire until 1818. Following the rise of the Maratha Empire, the palace became the center of Indian politics in the 18th century. The fort itself was largely destroyed in 1828 by an unexplained fire, but the surviving structures are now maintained as a tourist site.

Sinhagad Fort

It is located in the Pune district in Maharashtra. The fort is also one of the most famous forts in Maharashtra and has a great history associated with Tanaji Malusare. The fort was captured by Tanaji Malusare and his brother. Sinhagad (Lion's Fort) was strategically built to provide natural protection from enemies due to its very steep slopes. The walls of the forts and bastions were constructed only at key places. There are two gates to enter the fort named Kalyan Darwaja and Pune Darwaza. The Kalyan Darwaja is towards the southeast while the Pune Darwaza is towards the northeast.

Isckon Temple

The temple complex has two temples - the main Radha Krishna temple and the Venkateswara (Balaji) temple. The Radha Krishna temple is built in the North Indian architectural style using red stone and marble while the Venkateswara temple is built in the South Indian architectural style (similar to the Balaji temple in Tirumala) using Kota stone.
